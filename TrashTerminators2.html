Trash Terminators 2.0

Awards

2014- Lexus Eco Challenge Nationals First Place 2014- EPA ECO Video Challenge Nationals Second Place

The Team

  • We are the 'Trash Terminators 2.0'
  • Our team: Rohan Deshpande, Vincent Chen, Elizabeth Farmer, Quentin Sieredzki, and Graeme Zimmermann
  • School: Guy B Phillips Middle School, Chapel Hill, NC

The Problem

  • Our Chapel Hill landfill closed on July 1st 2013 and our trash is now transported more than 100 miles away to a new location
    • This is adding cost in transportation and in dumping fees
    • creates more pollution
  • Using a carbon footprint calculator, we found that for every ton of trash which our city will generate and ship to the new landfill, we will be releasing 57 pounds CO2 /ton of trash and spending $55/ton of trash.
  • Cafeteria trash audits conducted at other Chapel Hill Carrboro City Schools (CHCCS) showed that between 50-60% of trash is compostable.
  • 35% our city's trash is compostable based on Orange County Solid Waste Management data
  • By starting composting at all CHCCS schools and in our local community we will significantly:
    • Reduce greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide
    • Reduce emission of methane gas which is caused by food in landfill
    • Reduce the amount of money our county pays shipping the trash 100 miles away

Objectives

  • Make our school more Eco-Friendly
    • Increase the awareness of composting and sustainability in our school
    • Educate our School's staff and students
    • Start new composting initiatives
  • Duplicate our initiative in other Chapel Hill Carrboro City Schools (CHCCS)
    • Convince Mr. Schnitzer (CHCCS Sustainability director) to help us replicate our initiative in other schools in our district including 12 elementary and 3 other middle schools
  • Expand Community outreach and education
    • Use social media and other technologies to raise community awareness
    • Conduct an advertisement campaign to promote composting in our community
  • Propose to local and state government officials to start municipal composting

Implementation

We will impact the national/global community:

  • By making our school more eco-friendly
    • We will show that a school can significantly reduce trash that goes to the landfill
  • By duplicating our system in other schools
    • We will show that our initiative is replicable and scalable. Thus our school will act as a role model for schools across the nation
  • By conducting community outreach
    • We will provide a platform for educating the community at large
    • Show how social media can be used effectively to influence positive change in the society
  • By reaching out to the local and state government
    • We can provide evidence through quantifiable results in favor of municipal composting
    • Highlight the environmental and cost benefits of municipal composting
  • Our initiative is scalable at the global level
    • We plan to show our youtube videos on composting in schools in India and Myanmar

To make our school more Eco-Friendly we:

    • Created worm bins for students of ECO club
    • Created worm bins for classrooms around the school
    • Created new composting posters to hang around the school
    • Created and distributed Winter Holiday composting flyers
    • Whole Foods provided us with compostable trash bags
    • The bags were distributed to our teachers so they could compost over the holidays
    • We encouraged students and teachers to bring full compost bags back to the school to put in our compost bins
  • We created several different social media sites (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram)
  • We created an online survey on composting
    • We found out how much our community already knew about composting
    • We asked whether or not citizens wanted municipal composting
      • Results showed many citizens are interested in municipal composting

Communication

  • We commissioned an infomercial on composting to run on local TV
    • We used the money given to the school for winning regionals to create the infomercial
    • We provided the script, pictures, information and voice-over for the infomercial
    • It explains the benefits of composting and invites watchers to visit our website
    • It will air on local television channels (Animal Planet, Nickelodeon, Hallmark, ABC Family) from January through February
  • We presented to the Mayors and Town Councils of:
    • Chapel Hill
    • Carrboro (presentation rescheduled to Feb 4th due to weather)
    • We showed them results from our survey and our plans for municipal composting
  • Several different newspapers published articles about the Trash Terminators 2.0
    • These articles showed the work we had done and our next goals
    • These are the newspapers:
      • The Daily Tarheel (UNC Newspaper)
      • Falcon Flash (Our school newspaper)
      • The Chapel Hill News (Local Newspaper)
  • State Senator Foushee and (Former) Senator Kinnaird visited our school
    • We showed them our composting setup in the cafeteria
    • We are honored to have been mentioned in their weekly newsletters
  • We presented to the CHCCS Sustainability Director, Mr. Dan Schnitzer
    • We will work with him to implement composting at all schools in the district
    • He was very excited by the results of our project and its implications for CHCCS schools
  • Our work is now featured on social media such as Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram

Quantifiable Results

  • After our presentation to Mr. Schnitzer (CHCCS Sustainability Director), he agreed to have all the elementary and middle schools in our district to begin composting by the end of this school year
  • By diverting over 80% of the trash going to landfill by recycling and composting in all 16 CHCCS schools
    • We will divert 270,792 lbs of trash over 180 days of school
    • We will avoid 7,695 lb of CO2 going in our environment
    • We will save $7,425 in a school year on gas (by not shipping trash to landfill)
    • We will save $3120 if all schools in the district changed their dumpsters from eight yard dumpsters to six yard dumpsters
    • http://www.townofchapelhill.org/index.aspx?page=1142)
    • Results and more calculations
  • Social Media Site Counts as of January 26, 2014
    • We got over 94 likes on Facebook
    • We got over 400 hits on our Youtube channels
    • We got over 530 views on our website
    • These counts are increasing and we hope to achieve wide outreach through social media
